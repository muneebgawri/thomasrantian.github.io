I am currently a Phd student at UC Berkeley advised by Prof. Masayoshi Tomizuka. I hold a M.S. in Robotics and a B.S.E. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan. I also hold a B.S.E. in Mechanical Engineering from the Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

My research exploits tools from machine learning, control theory, and cognitive neuroscience to endow human-centered robots with the ability to: learn structured predictive models of human behaviors from demonstrations, plan effective motions to collaborate with and influence humans in uncertain environments, and plan safety-guaranteed motions to ensure safety when the human model is misspecified.

Outside school, I love fishing and diving. I am also a student pilot and a certified rescue diver.
