Motion planning for a humanoid robot

Motion planning

In this project, I implemented multiple sampling based motion planning algorithms (RRT, RRT*, Bidirectional RTT) for a humanoid robot to perform grasping tasks, and compared the performances of different methods. The red path is the raw path from the planer, and the blue path is the optimized and smoothed path.

Online footstep planning for humanoid robots

Motion planning

In this project, we proposed an online footstep planning algorithm for a humanoid robot. The algorithm plans an optimal (and collision free) step sequence for a humanoid robot to walk to the target location. The planning algorithm is based on Anytime A* algorithm, which finds an initial feasible solution first then sequentially optimizes the solution.

Human gesture recognition through skeleton estimation

Computer vision

A smooth operation of the autonomous vehicles is based on the interaction between autonomous vehicles and human pedestrians, while interpreting the human intentions remains as a challenge. Verbal communication and interpretation is one popular approach for human-robot interaction. Compared with verbal commutation, our method can deliver a faster and clearer intention without disturbed by the environment. We proposed a vision-based framework that identifies human gesture for autonomous vehicles. The framework synergies a human skeleton estimation model to find the locations of the human skeleton key points (hand, elbow, and etc.) and a gesture recognition model to identify certain gestures of interests based on the estimated human skeleton.

Simultaneous localization and mapping

ControlMotion planningComputer vision

In this project we implemented and tested a Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) algorithm to map an unknown environment. We implemented an odometry to track the robot’s location with the correction by the Monte-Carlo Localization algorithm. The motion of the robot is controlled by standard Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controller to follow a desired path. We also implemented the occupancy grid mapping algorithm to map the unknown environment, as well as the A* algorithm used for exploring the unknown environment. We integrated all the subsystems and deployed the robot to map the hall way of the basement of the Space Research Building.

Two-wheels balance robot

Control

In this project, we designed an on-board control system to stabilize a two-wheeled robotic system. We proposed a methodology that makes the robot balance itself while maintaining its ability to traverse and rotate. We implemented odometry to track the position of the robot and compared with ground truth recorded by Opti-track. We also proposed an online path planning algorithm that is used by our robot to navigate through a sequence of randomly generated gates.

Robotic arm grasping

In this project, we developed a robotic arm that is able to grasp and manipulate objects of interests. The robotic arm identifies the objects of interests through an attached camera, then plans trajectory to grasp the object and place the object to the desired location.
