About Me

I am currently a Phd student at UC Berkeley. I hold a M.S. in Robotics and a B.S.E. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan. I also hold a B.S.E. in Mechanical Engineering from the Shanghai Jiao Tong University. During my undergraduate and graduate studies, I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Ilya Kolmanovsky and Prof. Anouck Girard. I also spent time at WeRide.ai, Honda Research Institute, Qualcomm Research, and Baidu Research.

My research lies at the intersection of cognitive science, robotics, control theory, and artificial intelligence. My goal is to build safe and effective intelligence for autonomous robots that assist people in everyday life (such as self-driving cars and assistive robots). My general research interests are human-robot interaction and cognitive science-inspired machine intelligence. Specific topics include: shared autonomy; exploiting human cognitive models to facilitate human-robot interaction; life-long robot learning driven by prior experience, curiosity, social interaction, or meta-cognition; multi-modal representations learning.

Outside school, I love fishing and diving. I am also a student pilot and also a certified rescue diver.
